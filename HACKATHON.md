## Model Family

Gemma 4 spans three architectures tailored to different hardware:

| Variant | Size | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Small** | 2B and 4B effective parameter | Ultra-mobile, edge, and browser deployment (e.g., Pixel) |
| **Dense** | 31B parameter dense | Bridges server-grade performance with local execution |
| **Mixture-of-Experts** | 26B MoE | High-throughput, advanced reasoning |

> Judges will be looking for **intentional model selection** — show why your model was the right tool for the job.

## Challenge Prompts

### Build With Gemma 4

Build something useful or creative with any Gemma 4 model. The scope is wide open — IoT integrations, multimodal tools, long-context reasoning apps. What matters is that **Gemma 4 is doing real work at the heart of your project**, and that you make a clear case for why you chose the model you did.

**Judging Criteria**
- Intentional and effective use of Gemma 4
- Technical implementation and code quality
- Creativity and originality
- Usability and user experience

### Write About Gemma 4

Publish a post about Gemma 4 that educates, inspires, or sparks curiosity. There's no single right format — what matters is that your post offers something **genuine and useful** to the community.

**Suggested formats**
- **How-to guide** — setting up Gemma 4 locally, fine-tuning it, or integrating it into a real project
- **Comparison piece** — break down the three variants and help readers pick one
- **Personal essay / opinion** — what does a model this capable running locally mean for the future of AI?
- **Deep technical breakdown** — explore multimodal input, the 128K context window, or reasoning mode

> If you're primarily showing off a project, submit to **Build** instead. Each submission is eligible for one track only.

**Judging Criteria**
- Clarity and depth of explanation
- Originality of perspective or insight
- Practical value to the community
- Quality of writing

## FAQ

### Participation

**Can I submit to multiple prompts?**
Yes — you're welcome to submit to multiple prompts.

**Can one submission qualify for multiple prompts?**
No. Each submission is eligible for one prompt. To participate in both tracks, publish a separate post per track.

**Can I submit to a prompt more than once?**
Yes — multiple submissions per prompt are allowed, but each requires its own post.

**Can I work on a team?**
Team submissions (up to 4 people) are allowed for the **Build** prompt only. List your collaborators' DEV handles in your post so badges can be awarded to the whole team. Publish one submission per team. DEV does not handle prize-splitting — team members split prizes among themselves. **Write** is individual-only.

**How old do I have to be?**
18+.

**Eligibility by region?**
See the official challenge rules.

### Submission

**Can my submission include open-source code?**
Yes — riffing on existing work is encouraged, but your changes must be significant enough to make the submission valid. Transparency and license compatibility matter.

**When does riffing become plagiarism?**
Build on someone else's base to demonstrate your own ideas, but don't repackage the base. Judges need to see what you added — new functions, animations, sprites, presentation, etc. Just changing colors or one sprite isn't enough. Cite all non-trivial prior work.

**What happens if a submission is plagiarized or invalid?**
Incidental plagiarism → disqualification from the challenge (across all your submissions). Egregious plagiarism → suspension from DEV entirely.

**Do submissions have to be in English?**
Non-English submissions get the completion badge but aren't prize-eligible (judge-language limitation). English mastery is not judged — don't let language deter you.

**Do I need a license?**
Not required, but strongly recommended. Suggestions: MIT, Apache, BSD-2, BSD-3, Commons Clause.

**Can I use AI?**
Yes, as long as all other rules are followed.

### Judging and Prizing

**Tiebreakers?**
Highest number of positive reactions on the DEV post wins.

**How will I know if I won?**
Announcement DEV post on the winner-announcement date.

**When do I receive my badge?**
Usually the same day as the winner announcement.

**When do I receive my prize?**
The DEV Team contacts winners by email within 10 business days of the announcement.

**What's required to receive a cash prize?**
Winners (each team member) may need to sign and return an affidavit of eligibility plus a publicity/liability release, and provide tax filing info (W-9, SSN, or Federal Tax ID) within **7 business days** of first email notification.
